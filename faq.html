Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about GradeMate and how it works.

How does the 'Weighted Average' work?

Unlike a simple average, GradeMate multiplies your grade points by the "credits" of the subject. This means a 4-credit subject affects your GPA twice as much as a 2-credit subject.

How is 'Required SGPA' calculated?

This tool calculates the exact points gap between your current standing and your goal, then divides it by your upcoming credits to show you the minimum effort needed.

Do I need to sign up?

No, GradeMate is completely free and open for everyone. No login is required to use any of the tools.

Can I calculate GPA for older regulations?

This calculator follows the standard 10-point grading system (similar to R2021). Results might vary slightly for other regulations that use different grade points.

What happens if I refresh my browser?

Since we value your privacy and don't store data on any server, all your entered marks are stored locally in your browser (LocalStorage). Your data should survive a normal refresh as long as you aren't in private/incognito mode.

Is this an official university application?

No, this is a student-developed tool designed to help peers track their academic progress. Always verify with official university results.

Why do I see 'NA' in Expected Marks?

If you see 'NA', it means achieving that specific grade is mathematically impossible with your current internal marks (you would need to score more than 100 in the external exam).

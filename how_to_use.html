Understanding GPA Basics

Learn the fundamentals of academic grading and why it matters for your future.

What is SGPA & CGPA?

SGPA

Semester Grade Point Average measures your academic performance for a single semester. It's calculated based on the credits and grade points you earn in that specific semester.

Scale: 0-10 (10 is highest)

CGPA

Cumulative Grade Point Average is the weighted average of your SGPA across all completed semesters. It represents your overall academic performance throughout your degree.

Scale: 0-10 (10 is highest)

Why Calculate GPA?

Placements

Most companies have minimum CGPA cutoffs (e.g., 7.0, 7.5) for campus recruitment.

Higher Studies

Universities abroad require CGPA for admissions and scholarship considerations.

Scholarships

Merit-based scholarships often depend on maintaining a certain CGPA threshold.

Track Progress

Helps you monitor academic standing and set realistic goals for improvement.

How to Calculate?

Calculating SGPA

SGPA = Σ(Credits × Grade Points) / Σ(Total Credits)

Example: If you have 3 subjects with credits [4, 3, 2] and grade points [9, 8, 10], your SGPA = (4×9 + 3×8 + 2×10) / (4+3+2) = 86/9 = 9.56

Calculating CGPA

CGPA = Σ(SGPA × Sem Credits) / Σ(Total Credits)

Example: If Sem 1 SGPA = 8.5 (20 credits) and Sem 2 SGPA = 9.0 (22 credits), your CGPA = (8.5×20 + 9.0×22) / (20+22) = 368/42 = 8.76

The Math Explained

SGPA Formula

Weighted average of grade points earned in a semester.

SGPA = Σ(Credits × Grade Points) / Σ(Total Credits)

CGPA Formula

Weighted average of SGPA over all completed semesters.

CGPA = Σ(SGPA × Sem Credits) / Σ(Total Credits)

FAQ: How it Works

Weighted Average

Unlike a simple average, we multiply your grade points by the "credits" of the subject. This means a 4-credit subject affects your GPA twice as much as a 2-credit subject.

Required SGPA

This tool calculates the exact points gap between your current standing and your goal, then divides it by your upcoming credits to show you the minimum effort needed.

Do I need to sign up?

No, GradeMate is completely free and open for everyone. No login is required to use any of the tools.

Can I calculate GPA for older regulations?

This calculator follows the standard 10-point grading system (similar to R2021). Results might vary slightly for other regulations that use different grade points.

What happens if I refresh?

Since we value your privacy and don't store data on any server, all your entered marks are stored locally in your browser. However, hard refreshing might clear them.

Is this an official college app?

No, this is a student-developed tool designed to help peers track their academic progress. Always verify with official results.

Why 'NA' in Expected Marks?

If you see 'NA', it means achieving that specific grade is mathematically impossible with your current internal marks (you would need to score more than 100 in the external exam).
